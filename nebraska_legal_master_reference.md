## Part 1: Deepening the LPS Bond Investigation

We already know the 2020 bond promised no tax increase (but the overall levy went up), Northwest High is already over capacity, and they had to raise an extra $17M for athletics. Here is how we dig deeper to find actionable legal or regulatory violations:

### 1. The "Bait and Switch" Audit
*   **The Angle:** Did they use bond money for things *not* listed on the ballot?
*   **How to find it:** We need to file a public records request for the "Detailed General Ledger for the 2020 Bond Fund" from 2020 to present. We cross-reference every major vendor payment against the ballot language. If they paid for administrative salaries, software, or non-facility items out of the bond fund, that is illegal.

### 2. The Cost Overrun Cover-Up
*   **The Angle:** Did they know the high schools would cost more than budgeted before the election, but hid it to get the vote passed?
*   **How to find it:** We request the "Pre-Bond Architect Estimates and Feasibility Studies" from 2019. If the architects told LPS in 2019 that the schools would cost $350M, but LPS only asked voters for $290M and promised it was enough, that is securities fraud (misleading bond investors and taxpayers).

### 3. The Capacity Planning Failure
*   **The Angle:** Why is Northwest High already full? Was this gross negligence or intentional?
*   **How to find it:** We request the "Demographic and Enrollment Projections 2018-2020" used to justify the 1,000-student capacity. If the data clearly showed they needed a 1,500-student school, but they built a smaller one to keep the bond price artificially low, that is a massive breach of public trust.

## Part 2: The Property Tax Protest (Form 422) Walkthrough

Here is exactly how the property tax protest process works in Lancaster County, step-by-step.

### Finding Comps (Comparable Sales)
You do not need to pay an appraiser to find comps. You can do it yourself using public data.
1.  **The Official Source:** Go to the Lancaster County Assessor's website (`orion.lancaster.ne.gov`).
2.  **The Search:** Look up your property. Note your "Neighborhood Code," square footage, year built, and condition rating.
3.  **The Comps:** Search the database for other houses in your exact Neighborhood Code that sold in the last 12-24 months. Find 3-5 houses that are very similar to yours but sold for *less* than your assessed value, or have a lower assessed value per square foot.
4.  **The Alternative:** A local developer built a free tool specifically for Lancaster County: `LancasterPropertySearch.com`. It automatically pulls comps for you based on county data.

### Form 422: The Logistics
*   **The Fee:** There is **NO FEE** to file a property tax protest in Nebraska. It is completely free.
*   **Where to File:** You submit the form to the Lancaster County Clerk (555 S. 10th Street, Room 108, Lincoln, NE 68508). You can mail it, drop it off in person, or file it electronically through the county's online portal.
*   **What to Include:** The two-page Form 422, plus a printed spreadsheet or document listing your 3-5 comps and a short paragraph explaining the math (e.g., "My house is assessed at $150/sq ft, but these three identical houses on my street sold for $130/sq ft. My valuation should be lowered to match the market.").

### Can You File for Family?
*   **Yes, but with a catch:** Anyone can file a protest, but if you are not the legal owner of the property, you **must** attach a signed letter of authorization from the legal owner giving you permission to protest on their behalf. Without that letter, the county will dismiss the protest.

### What Happens After You File?
1.  **The Review:** An independent referee (usually a local appraiser hired by the county) will review your Form 422 and your comps.
2.  **The Hearing (Optional):** You can request a brief, in-person or phone hearing with the referee to explain your comps. It is highly recommended you do this.
3.  **The Decision:** The referee makes a recommendation to the County Board of Equalization. The Board votes on it in July.
4.  **The Result:** You will receive a letter in August. They will either lower your valuation, keep it the same, or (rarely) raise it.
5.  **The Appeal:** If you lose, you can appeal to the state Tax Equalization and Review Commission (TERC), but that costs a $40-$50 fee and is much more formal.

### Risk vs. Reward
*   **Risk:** The only real risk is your time. There is no filing fee, and it is extremely rare for them to raise your valuation just because you protested (unless you accidentally prove your house is worth *more*).
*   **Reward:** If you win, your property taxes are lowered for that year, and that lower baseline carries forward into future years, saving you thousands of dollars over time.

## Part 3: Nebraska Legal Deadlines (Statutes of Limitations)

If you are going to be initiating legal action or deep investigations, you must memorize these deadlines. If you miss them by one day, your case is dead.

| Action / Claim Type | Deadline (Statute of Limitations) | Nebraska Statute | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Property Tax Protest** | **June 1 - June 30** (Annually) | Neb. Rev. Stat. § 77-1502 | Must be filed within this exact 30-day window every year. |
| **Public Records Request Response** | **4 Business Days** | Neb. Rev. Stat. § 84-712 | The government must provide the records, or a written explanation of delay, within 4 days of receiving your request. |
| **Suing the Government (Tort Claim)** | **1 Year (Notice) / 2 Years (Suit)** | Neb. Rev. Stat. § 13-919 | You cannot just sue a school district or county. You MUST file a formal "Notice of Claim" with them within 1 year of the incident. You then have 2 years to file the actual lawsuit. |
| **Fraud / Misrepresentation** | **4 Years** | Neb. Rev. Stat. § 25-207 | The clock starts ticking when you *discover* the fraud, not when it happened. |
| **Breach of Written Contract** | **5 Years** | Neb. Rev. Stat. § 25-205 | Applies to formal, signed agreements. |
| **Libel / Slander (Defamation)** | **1 Year** | Neb. Rev. Stat. § 25-208 | Very short window if someone publishes false information about you. |
